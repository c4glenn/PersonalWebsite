Gold Rush Robotics competed in the **IEEE Southeastcon 2024 Hardware Competition** in Atlanta, Georgia.

## Custom PCB Design

The headline engineering work this year was a custom PCB I designed in KiCAD that served as the robot's central nervous system. The board bridged a **Teensy 4.0** microcontroller and an **Nvidia Jetson** over a **CAN-FD** bus, replacing ad-hoc wiring with a purpose-built board handling:

- CAN-FD transceiver circuitry for high-speed motor and sensor communication
- Power regulation and distribution for both the Jetson and embedded peripherals
- Onboard connectors for actuators, encoders, and I2C/SPI sensors

## From Competition to ROSCon

The CAN-FD transport layer I developed for this robot became the basis for a talk at **ROSCon 2024** in Odense, Denmark — demonstrating Micro-ROS running over CAN-FD as a general-purpose transport for embedded ROS2 nodes.
